# Credits for the example spectra

These spectra are real survey data, included here (unmodified apart from
repackaging and added header keywords) purely as demonstration inputs for
pyredshift. All credit belongs to the surveys and services below; please
follow their data-use and acknowledgment policies if you use these data
for anything beyond trying out this tool.

## DESI-elliptical-z0.49.fits

Luminous red galaxy at z = 0.4913, TARGETID 39628259121434302
(RA 18.2613°, Dec +20.0175°), from **DESI Data Release 1** (DESI
Collaboration). Retrieved via the **SPARCL** spectra service (Juneau et
al.) at NOIRLab's Astro Data Lab. DESI is managed by Lawrence Berkeley
National Laboratory with funding from the U.S. Department of Energy
Office of Science; see https://data.desi.lbl.gov/doc/acknowledgments/
for the full acknowledgment.

## SDSS-QSO-z1.97.fits

Quasar at z = 1.9726 (RA 150.6551°, Dec +27.0157°), from **SDSS/BOSS
Data Release 17** (SDSS Collaboration), repackaged into the standard
SDSS coadd table format. Retrieved via **SPARCL** at NOIRLab's Astro
Data Lab. Funding for SDSS has been provided by the Alfred P. Sloan
Foundation and participating institutions; see
https://www.sdss.org/collaboration/citing-sdss/ for the full
acknowledgment.

## JWST-SFG-z6.96.fits

Star-forming galaxy at z = 6.964, observed with the JWST/NIRSpec prism
by the **CAPERS** program (JWST GO 6368, PI M. Dickinson) in the EGS
field. The reduced spectrum (msaexp v4.4, original file
`capers-egs44-v4_prism-clear_6368_215739.spec.fits`) comes from the
**DAWN JWST Archive (DJA)**, maintained by the Cosmic Dawn Center,
which is funded by the Danish National Research Foundation (DNRF140);
reductions by G. Brammer et al. with the msaexp software
(https://github.com/gbrammer/msaexp). See
https://dawn-cph.github.io/dja/ for DJA data use guidelines.
